Driver unit economics

Per-day metrics use days the driver was on the road; per-trip metrics use paid (fare-bearing) trips — $0 trips are excluded from every per-trip average. Cost = Pay + Fuel + Insurance (Labor = Pay). Profit = Revenue − Cost (direct only; excludes overhead). Click any header to sort.

Shift report

Drag a trip onto another driver's row to reassign it. A trip moves as a unit (the drive-to-pickup Route + the Loaded leg together) and keeps its exact original time slot; the receiving driver's overlapping time is displaced to Wait, and the original driver's slot becomes Wait. If the new driver is more than 5 miles from the pickup at that time, you'll be asked to confirm. Click any block to flip it Off → Wait → back to original. Blocks are drawn to actual length (down to the minute); narrow ones are abbreviated R/L/W. Hover for pickup→dropoff. Net = revenue − labor (driver's hourly rate) − fuel (drive $6.25/hr, idle $1.98/hr) − insurance ($350/operating day, dropped if the van is parked all day). Sample days from RouteGenie, bounded to each driver's payroll clock-in/out (pre-shift and post-shift time excluded). Steve excluded (no route telemetry).
